AI automation for estate agents

Estate agents lose hours every week to admin that has nothing to do with selling or letting property: chasing documents, updating portals, re-keying the same details, progressing chains. AI automation can take a lot of that off your team, so they spend more time with clients and less time on paperwork.

By Rashid, founder of Layerform System Ltd. I build real property software.

The admin that eats an estate agency's week

It is rarely one big thing. It is a hundred small ones:

  • Chasing referencing documents, IDs, and signed paperwork.
  • Re-keying the same property and client details across your CRM and the portals.
  • Updating listings and sending the same status updates to vendors and buyers.
  • Sorting and replying to a flood of portal enquiries, most of which look the same.
  • Keeping a sale or let moving by chasing solicitors, references, and the chain.

What AI automation can do for an estate agency

Here is where AI and automation genuinely help, in plain terms:

  • Read and file documents. Pull the details out of referencing forms, IDs, and contracts automatically, and flag anything missing.
  • Chase on autopilot. Follow up on missing documents, references, and approvals on a schedule, without anyone remembering to.
  • Sort enquiries. Triage portal leads, spot the serious ones, and route them to the right negotiator.
  • Draft the routine messages. Prepare vendor updates and standard replies for a person to check and send.
  • Keep your systems in sync. Stop the same details being typed into your CRM and the portals separately.
